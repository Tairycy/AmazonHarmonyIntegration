Introduction

FLAC (Free Lossless Audio Codec) is an audio coding format for lossless compression of digital audio, and is also the name of the reference codec implementation. Digital audio compressed by the FLAC algorithm can typically be reduced to 50â€“60% of its original size and decompresses to an identical copy of the original audio data.

Features

  • Lossless decoder
  • Supports native FLAC format streaming
  • No theoretical limitation on maximum Sample rate and bitrate

Note: 
Lossless decoding will consume large buffer size in runtime, therefore, refer to the FLAC performance table to determine whether it fits in your application.
